Namib-Naukluft National Park

The Namib-Naukluft Park is a national park in western Namibia situated between the coast of the Atlantic Ocean and the edge of the Great Escarpment.

The best known area of the park and one of the main visitor attractions in Namibia is Sossusvlei, a clay pan surrounded by sand dunes, and Sesriem, a small canyon of the Tsauchab.

Location and description

With an overall area of 49,768 km, the Namib-Naukluft National Park was at the time of its last expansion the largest game park in Africa and the fourth largest in the world.

Namib means Open Space, and the Namib Desert gave its name to form Namibia, land of open spaces.

Wildlife

A surprising collection of creatures survives in the hyper-arid region, including:

  • Snakes are elongated, limbless, carnivorous reptiles of the suborder Serpentes.

  • Geckos are small lizards found in warm climates throughout the world.

  • Gemsbok are large antelope native to the arid regions of Southern Africa.

Geography

The region is characterised by high, isolated inselbergs and kopjes, made up of dramatic granites, feldspars, and sandstone.

History

The park was established in 1907 when the German Colonial Administration proclaimed three game reserves in German South West Africa.

  1. Namib-Naukluft park was proclaimed as Game Reserve No. 3.

  2. Etosha National Park is a national park in northwestern Namibia.
